What is Chicken Curry?

Chicken Curry is a popular dish made by cooking chicken in a flavorful gravy of tomatoes, onions, garlic, ginger, and a variety of spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The dish is known for its rich, savory, and slightly spicy flavors. It is commonly served with rice, bread, or naan, and is a favorite in many regions of South Asia and around the world.

Origin of Chicken Curry

The origin of Chicken Curry can be traced to the Indian subcontinent, where it has evolved into various regional styles. The dish spread to many parts of the world through colonization and trade, becoming a staple in many countries. It has numerous variations, each adding its own touch of local ingredients and cooking techniques.

How to make Chicken Curry:

Ingredients:

  • 500g Chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 medium Onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 Tomatoes (pureed or chopped)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2-3 tbsp Oil or Ghee
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Spices

  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
  • 1/2 tsp Cumin Powder
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala

Preparation:

  • Wash and cut chicken into medium pieces.
  • Finely chop onions and puree or chop tomatoes.
  • Keep all spices ready and fresh coriander chopped for garnish.

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  4. Add all dry spices (turmeric, chili, coriander, cumin) and mix well.
  5. Add tomatoes and cook until the oil separates from the mixture.
  6. Add chicken pieces, mix to coat, and cook until sealed.
  7. Add water as needed, cover and simmer until chicken is fully cooked and tender.
  8. Sprinkle garam masala and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice, roti, or naan.
